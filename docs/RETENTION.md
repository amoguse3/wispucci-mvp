# Retention — Keeping Gen Z Learners

Research on what keeps Gen Z engaged (2026), and how Wispucci applies it.

## The hard facts

- 32% of Gen Z report app fatigue, deleting more than they install.
- They install ~20% fewer apps but open survivors ~20% more often. An install is the start of a 72-hour audition.
- They abandon after ~3.7 seconds of friction.
- Average freemium conversion is 2-5%; top quartile hits 10-25% through free-tier architecture, not generosity.

## What works

### Try before signup (aha moment under 5 minutes)
Notion, Figma, Linear activate users BEFORE signup.

**In the app:** user learns immediately, no account. After finishing a topic, a soft skippable signup appears. Progress saved locally until signup.

### Strategic friction (not frictionless)
A little effort builds stronger habits and higher LTV.

**In the app:** pretests and completion problems require real thought.

### In-app engagement > push/email
AI filters push and email. Everything lives inside the app.

**In the app:** streak, progress bars, next-topic auto-advance.

### Bite-sized, one action per screen
**In the app:** single-screen lessons, no scroll, advance by button.

### Game design principles (not fake gamification)
BITKRAFT 2026: EdTech + AI + game design = top vertical. Adaptive, rewarding, relevant.

**In the app:** adaptive difficulty, orb reactions, celebrations tied to real progress.

### Flexible streaks (critical for neurodivergent learners)
UBC 2026: rigid don't-break-the-chain systems deepen shame and hurt ADHD/anxious users.

**In the app:** streak counts recent activity, no punishment for a missed day.

### Social / leaderboards, carefully
Optional only; prefer cooperative mechanics over pure ranking.

**Planned:** opt-in leaderboards + co-op study.

## The differentiator

Competitors (Notiqo, TesteBac) only test. Wispucci teaches: adaptive lessons, guess-first, faded guidance, spaced harder review, a companion that explains and highlights. Test is a moment; teaching is the process.
